STEP 3 - Using LLMs to produce new knowledge

Prompt 1

First of all, we needed a description of the Fontana del Nettuno, to get more historical information and details about it.
We tried to use the zero-shot prompting technique by asking:

Could you give me a brief description of the “Fontana del Nettuno” in Bologna, one of Giambologna’s artworks, containing historical information and some details about it?

According to this technique, the prompt contains no examples or demonstrations, and in this case we can use it because to get more information about our topic we do not need to explain anything.

Gemini:









Analysis:

  • So both Gemini and ChatGPT agree on the fact that the fountain has been a symbol of the city of Bologna since its creation and that’s also something we could verify with our previous results.
  • ChatGPT gives a more accurate description of the elements of the fountain and their meaning (for example, the four cherubic figures representing four important rivers of the world). It also tells us about the height of the Neptune statue (3.2 meters) and the material the fountain is made of (bronze and marble). It also defines Giambologna’s style as Mannerist. Gemini, on the other hand, gives us some details but, all in all, it provides a less accurate description of the artwork.
  • Both LLMs agree on the fact that the fountain is a symbol of the power of papacy and of the city’s allegiance to the Papal States, which is a new important detail for us.
  • They both agree on the fact that the fountain was commissioned in 1563.
  • Gemini provides us with the start and completion date of the work. However, we wanted to verify this data and according to this article of LA STAMPA the true completion date of the work would be 1566 (and not 1567). Therefore Gemini is not correct.
  • They both provide some information on who commissioned the fountain. And they’re both right, in the sense that the fountain was commissioned by the Pope, by the Bolognese Senate (Gemini) and by the legate Cardinal Borromeo (ChatGPT).
  • However, according to the website of the Comune di Bologna the creation of the statue was also wanted by the vicelegate Cardinal Pier Donato Cesi, who promoted the architectural renovation of the center of Bologna. So there is an important name missing: let’s see if we can get his name by further questioning the LLMs.

    • Prompt 4.1

    After having built a discussion with them on the topic, we then asked the LLMs to transform some of the information we got about Laureti into RDF format, using the Arco ontology. By doing so we employed a mix of the Zero-shot and of the Chain-of-Thought Prompting Technique.
    The Chain-of-thought prompting is a technique used in LLMs to improve their ability to perform complex reasoning tasks. It involves breaking down a complex problem into smaller, more manageable steps, and then guiding the LLM through each step sequentially. In this specific case, we simplified it by combining it with the Zero-shot prompting technique: we asked a zero-shot question in the previous step, Prompt 4, and then took some of the information from that answer to build a subsequent, more complex question.

    The question we asked for this prompt was the following:

    Tommaso Laureti was an Italian painter and architect who lived from approximately 1530 to 1602. He is known for his work on the Fontana del Nettuno in Bologna, where he designed the overall structure of the fountain, including the base, surrounding elements, and waterworks systems. Q: Could you transform this description into RDF format and use Arco ontology?

As the website of the Patrimonio Culturale dell’Emilia Romagna confirms, there is a statue of Mercurio by Giambologna preserved at the Museo Civico Medievale in Bologna, which ChatGPT confirmed.
