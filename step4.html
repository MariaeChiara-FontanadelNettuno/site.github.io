STEP 4 - Designing triples

STEP 4.1 - Triples based on ArCo

We decided to design triples based on the most important information about our topic, retrieved through searching the ArCo ontology.
We also created them as a way to practice and understand how to design triples in order to design new ones later.
In addition, the following triples are useful for finding the most relevant properties and values of our artwork without having to run a SPARQL query again.

TRIPLES - SET 1

Since the Neptune Fountain in Bologna is a public monument, we were only able to find representations of it.
For this reason, in the following series of triples, we chose the IRI of a photo of the fountain, as it is one of the closest representations of the artwork we could find.

riple 1 – a-cd:hasAuthor
Subject: https://w3id.org/arco/resource/PhotographicHeritage/0800633982
Predicate: hasAuthor
Object (Giambologna): https://w3id.org/arco/resource/Agent/a17358b654a5aa1879cec8969f6b39c1

Triple 2 – a-cd:hasSubject

Predicate: hasSubject

STEP 4.2 - Triples to enrich ArCo

We then tried to design new triples based on the information extracted from the LLMs.
As pointed out earlier, we noted that the most relevant details with which to enrich the ArCo KG are:
- who commissioned the Neptune Fountain (we focused on the main patrons mentioned by the LLMs: Pope Pius IV and the Bolognese Senate);
- information about the architect Tommaso Laureti, the second author of the fountain;
- other works by Giambologna preserved in Bologna (the most important of which is the statue of Mercurio).
Again, we took the photo (Negativo) by Anonimo as a representation of the fountain (as subject/object), as it is one of the closest representation to the artwork we have found.
